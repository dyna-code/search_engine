Tech Billionaires Have Added An Astonishing $750 Billion To Their Fortunes Over The Past Year

The world’s wealthiest technology moguls have ridden the AI-fueled frenzy to never-before-seen heights.

By Phoebe Liu, Forbes Staff

There’s rich, and there’s really rich. The planet’s technology tycoons are increasingly members of the latter camp. Forbes found 342 billionaires with fortunes in tech on this year’s World’s Billionaires list, up from 313 last year. As a group, they are worth a combined $2.6 trillion, more than any other industry and an astounding $750 billion more than in 2023, a bigger gain than any other industry.

Nobody had a better year than Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg, whose net worth has increased by more than any other billionaire, up by nearly $113 billion over the past 12 months, to an estimated $177 billion. Meta’s 182% rally catapulted Zuckerberg, who owns 14% of the company, from the 16th-richest to fourth-richest on Forbes’ billionaire ranking, his highest spot ever. What Zuckerberg branded Meta’s “year of efficiency”—including layoffs, cost-cutting and focusing on its advertising and core businesses—made investors cheer. The company has grown up, entering its 20th year and paying out its first-ever dividend. Of course, Zuckerberg is also investing heavily in AI, telling Forbes in 2023 that “AI will go across everything” at Meta.

Of course the biggest reason behind the tech rally is the artificial intelligence craze, as investors rush into companies building AI products or selling products to AI companies. The poster child of the boom, Nvidia cofounder and CEO Jensen Huang, had a banner year. Shares of graphics chip-maker Nvidia soared, landing Huang among the world’s 20 richest people for the first time—up from No. 78 last year. He’s worth an estimated $77 billion. Another Nvidia shareholder, founding board member Harvey Jones, became a billionaire this year on the back of the soaring stock, one of several new AI moguls to make the Forbes list.

U.S. tech giants are getting in on the action, too, investing heavily in AI’s large language models and the processing power they require, exciting investors and making their founders even richer. Microsoft has inked partnerships with OpenAI and Inflection AI, Amazon and Alphabet have invested heavily in Anthropic AI and Alphabet developed its own model, Gemini. Several associated billionaires—Bill Gates, Jeff Bezos, Michael Dell, Steve Ballmer, Larry Page, Sergey Brin and Larry Ellison—are all at least $20 billion richer than they were last year. (Elon Musk, the world’s second richest person, does employ a lot of technology in his most valuable holding, Tesla, but is not included among the tech billionaires because we classify him as an automotive billionaire.)

THE $750 BILLION YEAR

The world’s richest tech tycoons added a combined $750 billion to their fortunes since last year. All but Zhang Yiming and Ma Huateng are richer. Here’s who gained the most. (Values are in billions of U.S. dollars. Net worths are as of March 10, 2023 in light blue and March 8, 2024 in dark blue.)


Most of this year’s biggest tech gainers don’t even work day-to-day for the companies that are driving their ever-increasing wealth. Of the ten richest tech billionaires this year, only three—Zuckerberg, Huang and Dell—are still CEOs of their respective companies. Bezos stopped being Amazon’s CEO in 2021. That same year, ByteDance’s Zhang Yiming resigned from both his CEO and chairman posts, although he’s still a major shareholder. Page and Brin are four years out from day-to-day operations at Alphabet, while Gates left the Microsoft board in 2020 and Ballmer stepped down as the Microsoft CEO in 2014. (Gates did tell Forbes last year that he spends about 10% of his time meeting with various teams at Microsoft. And others, such as Page and Brin, oversee their businesses as board members.)

Here are the 10 richest people in tech and how they have fared over the past 12 months.

NET WORTHS ARE AS OF MARCH 8, 2024, COMPARED WITH MARCH 10, 2023.

10. Zhang Yiming

Net worth: $43 Billion | Change vs. 2023: -$1.6 Billion | Source of wealth: ByteDance

The ByteDance founder is one of just two of this year’s richest 20 tech billionaires who’s poorer than he was a year ago. Even though he stepped down as ByteDance’s chairman in 2021, reportedly under pressure from the Chinese government, he’s still a major shareholder. His fortune has taken a hit along with ByteDance’s valuation, amid speculation that its major holding, TikTok, may be banned in the U.S. or that ByteDance may have to divest.

9. Jensen Huang

Net worth: $77 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$56 Billion | Source of wealth: Nvidia

There’s much to “envy” about the cofounder and CEO of the company named after its Latin word, invidia. Nvidia has had an astounding year, with its share price up 300%. Huang, CEO and 3% shareholder, is the richest he’s ever been—and among the world’s 20 richest people, ranked No. 20 overall, for the first time ever. Still, “no task is beneath me,” Huang said in a March interview at Stanford University. “I’ve cleaned more toilets than all of you combined.”

8. Michael Dell

Net worth: $91 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$41 Billion | Source of wealth: Dell Technologies

Dell’s big year was characterized by one of the largest acquisitions in history. In November, Broadcom acquired Dell Technologies spinoff VMware—of which Michael Dell owned 40%—for $69 billion in cash and stock, an approximately $30 billion dollar payday for the Dell founder and CEO. Around the same time, he also donated more than $3 billion to his family foundation, a combination of cash, Dell stock and Broadcom stock that would certainly lessen Dell’s hefty tax bill.

7. Sergey Brin

Net worth: $110 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$34 Billion | Source of wealth: Google

The Google cofounder reportedly came out of semi-retirement and submitted code changes to Google’ AI chatbot models last year—and was listed as a “core contributor” to Gemini when the model was released in December. Brin, who has a genetic mutation making him more likely to get Parkinson’s, gave another $150 million in 2023 to research for a cure for the degenerative disease; he’s now given $1.25 billion to the cause.

6. Larry Page

Net worth: $114 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$35 Billion | Source of wealth: Google

Page and his Google cofounder Brin are now five years removed from day-to-day operations at the tech giant, although they’re still the company’s biggest shareholders. After unloading $2.5 billion (pre-tax) from mid-2021 to mid-2022, Page has not sold a share since. He has mostly stayed away from public view, spending time on his five reported private islands, including one in Fiji and one in Puerto Rico.

5. Steve Ballmer

Net worth: $121 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$40 Billion | Source of wealth: Microsoft

The Los Angeles Clippers owner will move his team—the NBA’s fifth-most valuable—to a new stadium this summer: the (privately financed) $2 billion Intuit Dome, near LAX airport. Ballmer’s fortune surged by $40 billion since last year, due in part to the Clippers’ higher value but mostly thanks to soaring Microsoft shares.

4. Bill Gates

Net worth: $128 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$24 Billion | Source of wealth: Microsoft

Despite being nearly the wealthiest he’s ever been, Gates ranks the lowest he has since 1992 thanks to stiff competition at the top, a costly 2021 divorce and the $59 billion he has given to charity. The Microsoft cofounder was the world’s richest person for 18 of the 23 years from 1995 to 2017.

3. Larry Ellison

Net worth: $141 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$34 Billion | Source of wealth: Oracle

A decade after stepping down as Oracle’s CEO, Ellison is still chairman, chief technology officer and its largest shareholder, with 40% of the company. Oracle stock is up 34%, and it threw off more than $1 billion (pretax) in dividends to Ellison over the last 12 months, helping make him $34 billion richer. His other ventures aren’t faring as well: His stake in X is worth less than a third of the $1 billion he paid for it, and Project Ronin, a cancer software startup he cofounded in 2018, is shutting down.

2. Mark Zuckerberg

Net worth: $177 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$113 Billion | Source of wealth: Facebook

What a run it’s been for the Meta CEO. After the social media giant’s stock plunged 75% from its 2021 peak, Zuckerberg laid off almost a quarter of its staff while maintaining huge bets on AI and the metaverse. Shares nearly tripled over the past year, making Zuck, at 39, the wealthiest he has ever been.

1. Jeff Bezos

Net worth: $194 Billion | Change vs. 2023: +$80 Billion | Source of wealth: Amazon

The Amazon founder struck gold this year, in terms of both wealth and sunshine. He’s $80 billion richer, thanks to a 93% jump in his e-commerce leviathan’s stock. Meanwhile, he spent $150 million buying homes on Miami’s “billionaire bunker” island as part of a move to sunny, tax-friendly Florida after three decades in drizzly Seattle.
