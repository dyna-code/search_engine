Elon Musk's SpaceX Inspiration4 launch successful

Billionaires Musk, Bezos and Branson are racing for space supremacy

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Comments
  • Print
  • Email
By Breck Dumas FOXBusiness
close
video

SpaceX launches world's first all-civilian human spaceflight

SpaceX launches world's first all-civilian human spaceflight

Elon Musk's SpaceX and its civilian crew made history with their successful Wednesday evening launch, and the Inspiration4 will now spend the next three days orbiting Earth.

The company's first private spaceflight and the world's first all-civilian spaceflight took off from Launch Complex 39A at NASA's Kennedy Space Center in Florida at 8:02pm ET to a cheering crowd of spectators that included Musk.

The billionaire CEO of Shift4 Payments, Jared Isaacman, who is an accomplished pilot and commander of the Inspiration4, is funding the mission. He is joined by St. Jude Children's Research Hospital physician assistant Hayley Arceneaux, geoscientist Dr. Sian Proctor, and data engineer Chris Sembroski, who is a U.S. Air Force veteran and St. Jude donor.

SPACEX TO MAKE HISTORY WITH ALL-CIVILIAN CREW MISSION, INSPIRATION4

Isaacman came up with the idea for the Inspiration4 mission, and it is being used in part of a broader fundraising campaign aimed at raising $200 million for the children's hospital. The billionaire has not revealed how much he paid SpaceX for the mission.
