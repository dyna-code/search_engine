Costco rumored to be discontinuing sweet treat from food court

Rumors swirling that churros will be phased out in the new year

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Comments
  • Print
  • Email
By Pilar Arias FOXBusiness
close
video

Check out what's clicking on FoxBusiness.com

Costco Wholesale is planning to discontinue the churro from its food court menu, according to social media users.

Rumors about the change started swirling on Reddit, according to The U.S. Sun. 

"My brother (Costco employee) just told me they were getting rid of the churros at the food court and replacing them with cookies," a user near the Goleta, California, warehouse said.

The twisted, doughy cinnamon-sugar treats can currently be purchased for $1.49 at club food courts.  

COSTCO'S FOOD COURTS ARE BRINGING BACK THIS FAN-FAVORITE HOT DOG TOPPING

Customers wait in line to order at a Costco food court outside a Costco Wholesale Corp. store in Hawthorne, California, on June 14, 2022. (PATRICK T. FALLON/AFP via Getty Images / Getty Images)

Despite the fact that the company has not made an official announcement on the swap, another Reddit user chimed in on the thread with insider knowledge.

"It is true. They have found a company to make the cookies, agreements are finalized. They will be showing up in early 2024, along with chocolate ice cream which will appear before summer," the user wrote.

CUSTOMERS CAN'T BELIEVE WHAT COSTCO IS CHARGING FOR THIS NEW FOOD COURT ITEM

Many long churros on display showing texture closeup. (iStock / iStock)

The possibility of losing the churro as a dessert option has sparked mixed feelings in the post thread.

"They taste like sugar cardboard. They should phase them out and replace them with pretzels," one Reddit user wrote.

"[I] hope not. thats my fav treat there for 20 years. i WILL complain," another responded.

Costco has not put out a statement about discontinuing churros from its food courts. (iStock / iStock)

Some users say the food item will not be missed, while others say they tasted better prior to the COVID-19 pandemic or when the store used a former recipe. 

Others suggested that the retail giant should bring back soft pretzels, or other sweet treat options.

Costco did not immediately respond to a Fox Business request for comment. 

Sam's Club, one of Costco's biggest competitors, offers both pretzels and churros in its cafés.
